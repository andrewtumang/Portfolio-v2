Room Layout Planning App Prototype

This project involved research, planning, designing, and prototyping of mobile application.

My Role: UX/UI Designer
Tools Used: Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, InVision, Miro/RealTimeBoard, Trello
Timeframe: 2 weeks


The Problem:


When faced with an empty room, one may not know how to furnish it. Not everyone is an interior designer and not everyone plans out their rooms down to the details, which results in sometimes messy and inconsistent layouts.


The Solution

Inhance is a platform that helps users bring to life a vision for their living space. By incorporating planning, shopping, and sharing into one app, users can experience a simpler way to interior design.


User Research

User interviews and surveys were conducted in order to gain insight into how users plan their room layouts and what specific tools they use to do so.

Results showed that a majority of users actually have a vision or plan of some sort for a layout, but do not plan it out in detail. Users either used pen and paper to plan or no tool at all, suggesting that there is a need for a room layout planning tool. Additionally, some users had a lack of experience in interior design, and would either seek assistance or only buy essentials for their rooms.


User Persona

Using data from the interviews and survey responses, the following persona was generated to stay oriented towards user needs and frustrations throughout the design process. This persona represents a user who is moving into a new apartment for his first time, but is unaware of how he should design the layout.



Competitor Analysis

The main competitors consisted of the website Floor Planner, the apps amikasa and IKEA Place, and regular pen and paper. Based on user research, pen and paper was used the most, followed by IKEA Place. The other competitors were not used, either due to lack of knowledge about them or their costs. The goal of Inhance is to provide as many features as possible while being at least as accessible as IKEA Place by providing free services.

User Testing

User testing was conducted with the goal of determining whether or not the planning and shopping process was intuitive. However, users were confused about the layout of the Home page and about the split user flows between planning and shopping.
